About Alpha Delta Phi

The Alpha Delta Phi Fraternity was founded in 1832 at Hamilton College in Clinton, New York by Samuel Eells (1810-1842). Founded as a literary society, it evolved into one of the most distinguished of the original American college fraternities. It has retained its focus on its literary roots, by attracting only the best students at only the more prestigious colleges and universities in the United States and Canada.

Our chapters are leaders on their respective compuses, where some of our chapter houses have been designated architectural or historic landmarks.

The Alpha Delta Phi experience has helped a disproportionate number its 50,000 lifelong members to become top leaders in industry, government, education, and religion.
